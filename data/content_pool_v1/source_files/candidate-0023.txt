I've been an AI engineer for the past ten years. At the start is was building custom model architectures and training them. That quickly changed to modifying and fine tuning off the shelf models. Now it is all LLM api calls. The LLMs have gotten excellent at writing code in the past year. This time last year I thought they where a neat assistant but to unreliable for anything outside of writing well defined code blocks. Now with a bit of oversight LLMs can generate code bases that would have taken teams of devs a year in just a few days. At this pace of innovation software won't be a field in another 5 years, or will look so drastically different. The models and work flows aren't good enough just yet to replace me. Another year of innovation at this pace and I'll be out of a job for sure. That still doesn't stop the execs from thinking they can replace everyone with AI today. We have had huge team cuts. The workload and expectations are crazy. Execs still expect us to do way more with less. Most of my coworkers are maliciously complying. They are just rubber stamping all this AI code into production. The company wide experiment with AI-DLC showed that stability was reduced, and achieved less. The VPs take away was that we just didn't do enough AI-DLC. LLMs have sucked all the fun out of this career. The deep problem solving, white board coding sessions, planning. All I do all day now is attend meetings and manage coding agents. Execs don't really care about anything but buzz. We are all so burnt out, a lot of our friends were laid off.
